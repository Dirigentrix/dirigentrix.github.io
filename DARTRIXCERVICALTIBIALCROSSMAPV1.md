## 🟣 SYSTEM PREMISE
The neck (cervical plexus) and the shin (tibial/calf segment) form the two opposite poles of a single, continuous kinematic and fluid transmission chain. In the DARTRIX system, this map bridges the Upper Respiratory Helix (cervical, phrenic, GB22) and the Lower Fluid Helix (KI3-KI7, femoral triangle, venous flow).

---

## 🟦 1. KINEMATIC CHAIN SPECS
The transmission of physical force and directional vectors flows along the following structural cascade:
$$\vec{v}_{HEAD}(t) \rightarrow \vec{v}_{SPINE}(t) \rightarrow \vec{v}_{PELVIS}(t) \rightarrow \vec{v}_{KNEE}(t) \rightarrow \vec{v}_{SHIN}(t)$$

Where:
*   `\vec{v}_{HEAD}`: Global spatial orientation and cervical angle (governs where the body looks and commits).
*   `\vec{v}_{SHIN}`: The terminal vector of force transmission entering the ground, ball, or field of action.

---

## 🟩 2. THE THREE AXES OF TRANSMISSION
*   **AXIS 1: HEADVECTOR -> SHINVECTOR**
    *   Cervical flexion (head down) shifts the global gravity center forward, forcing pelvic tilting, altering knee rotation, and forcing the tibia into a compensatory angle.
*   **AXIS 2: RESPHELIX -> LOWERHELIX**
    *   The respiratory rhythm of the upper chest (phrenic suction) directly drives the fluid return velocity from the deep calf and shin vectors (KI3-KI7).
*   **AXIS 3: POSTURELOAD -> VENOUSFLOW**
    *   Forward head posture and neck stiffness lock the cervical spine, lowering breathing amplitude, which compromises the calf muscle pump and stalls venous return in the lower leg.
